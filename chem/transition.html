introduction

Traditionally, we are usually only taught the more important metals, such as alkali or alkaline, or those in the first 20 to 30, or something more important like gold.

Unfortunately, this leaves other metals in the dust, and they are usually left out in the curriculum. But, in my opinion, they are a fascinating world to discover, and you may not even realize just how much of those we're using in daily life.

This includes transition metals, which are defined as metals in the groups 3 through 10, as well as a number of metals in groups 11 to 13, plus the lanthanides and actinides, which are those in the bottom 2 rows separated from the main chart in your periodic table.

Hence, as a token of appreciation to these often forgotten metals, let's talk about them here.

For the sake of ordering, we'll go through the more commonly heard metals first, then the ones you'll sometimes hear about, and finally the rarer (literally) ones.
